Taj Mahal – Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar – A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, this 73-meter tall minaret was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193 and is the tallest brick minaret in the world.

Red Fort – Located in Delhi, this massive red sandstone fort was built by Emperor Shah Jahan and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.

India Gate – A war memorial in New Delhi built in honor of Indian soldiers who died in World War I. It resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris.

Hawa Mahal – Known as the "Palace of Winds," this pink sandstone structure in Jaipur, Rajasthan, has 953 small windows (jharokhas) designed to allow air circulation.

Gateway of India – A grand archway in Mumbai, Maharashtra, built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves – Located in Maharashtra, these rock-cut caves feature Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain temples, with intricate carvings and paintings dating back to 200 BCE.

Konark Sun Temple – A stunning 13th-century temple in Odisha, dedicated to the Sun God, Surya, built in the shape of a giant chariot with intricately carved wheels and horses.

Charminar – An iconic structure in Hyderabad, Telangana, built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1591, known for its four grand arches and minarets.

Meenakshi Temple – A magnificent temple in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar, known for its towering gopurams (temple towers).

Mysore Palace – A grand royal palace in Karnataka, famous for its Indo-Saracenic architecture and the grand Dasara celebrations.

Victoria Memorial – A white marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, built in memory of Queen Victoria, showcasing Indo-Saracenic and Mughal architectural styles.

Brihadeeswarar Temple – A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, this 11th-century temple was built by Raja Raja Chola and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Sanchi Stupa – Located in Madhya Pradesh, this ancient Buddhist stupa was built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE and is an important pilgrimage site.

Golconda Fort – A historic fort in Hyderabad, Telangana, known for its acoustics and diamond trade, including the famous Kohinoor diamond.

Rani Ki Vav – A stepwell in Patan, Gujarat, known for its intricate carvings and seven levels of sculpted panels, built by Queen Udayamati in the 11th century.

Jantar Mantar – An astronomical observatory in Jaipur, Rajasthan, built by Maharaja Jai Singh II, featuring the world's largest stone sundial.

Fatehpur Sikri – A Mughal city near Agra, Uttar Pradesh, built by Emperor Akbar, featuring grand structures like Buland Darwaza and Jama Masjid.

Humayun’s Tomb – A beautiful Mughal mausoleum in Delhi, built in memory of Emperor Humayun, which later inspired the design of the Taj Mahal.

Kumbhalgarh Fort – A massive fort in Rajasthan with the second-longest continuous wall in the world (after the Great Wall of China), built by Rana Kumbha.

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan citadel located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It is famous for its breathtaking views and well-preserved stone architecture.

Petra is a historic archaeological site in Jordan, known as the "Rose City" due to its rock-cut architecture and pink sandstone cliffs.

Chichen Itza is a large Mayan city in Mexico, home to the famous El Castillo pyramid, which was used for astronomical observations.

Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower in Paris, France, and one of the most iconic landmarks in the world.

Statue of Liberty in New York, USA, was a gift from France and symbolizes freedom and democracy.

Stonehenge in England is a prehistoric monument made of massive stone circles, believed to have been used for religious or astronomical purposes.

The Pyramids of Giza in Egypt are one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, built as tombs for the pharaohs.

Angkor Wat in Cambodia is the largest religious monument in the world, originally built as a Hindu temple before becoming a Buddhist site.

Hagia Sophia in Turkey has served as a cathedral, mosque, and museum, showcasing stunning Byzantine architecture.

The Leaning Tower of Pisa in Italy is famous for its unintended tilt due to unstable foundation soil.

Alhambra in Spain is a stunning palace and fortress complex, showcasing Islamic architecture from the Moorish period.

Neuschwanstein Castle in Germany is a fairy-tale-like castle that inspired Disney’s Sleeping Beauty Castle.

Mount Rushmore in the USA features the faces of four American presidents carved into a mountain in South Dakota.

Acropolis of Athens in Greece is an ancient citadel that includes the Parthenon, dedicated to the goddess Athena.

Forbidden City in Beijing, China, was the imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties and is now a museum.

Moai Statues on Easter Island, Chile, are giant stone figures carved by the Rapa Nui people.

Edinburgh Castle in Scotland sits atop Castle Rock and has been a royal residence for centuries.

Borobudur in Indonesia is the world's largest Buddhist temple, featuring intricate carvings and stupas.

Tower of London in England has served as a fortress, prison, and royal residence throughout history.

Saint Basil’s Cathedral in Moscow, Russia, is known for its colorful onion-shaped domes and unique architecture.
